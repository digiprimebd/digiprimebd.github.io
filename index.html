No IX was styled in old traditional fantasy look i meaning the standard characters we use, medieval world, magic system and airships setup, similar to I-VI context, where VIII and VII was futuristic sci fi style world with modern systems like guns and etc. I'm referring to standard Final Fantasy identity, not art style, since FFXV is more akin to an urban fantasy look. Also FFXII & FFXIV ARR used a high fantasy medieval style world too, effectively creating the split within the titles for the types of themes the individual games use. (Sci Fi vs Medieval Fantasy)

It goes heavily back and forth between "High Fantasy" and "Sci-Fi Fantasy". FF1-6 are the traditional games, FF7 is heavily industrialised sci-fi and FF8 took that a bit more modern but introduced spaceships element as a later point, FFIX was a direct homage to the pre VII games. FFX/X2 went to a more unique style similar to Blitzpunk. FFXI, FFXII & FFXIV returned to medieval fantasy settings, where FFXIII returned to the FFX style Blitzpunk setting but far future tech with FFXV and FFXVI going towards more of a realism approach based on an Urban and European setting. As of right now, most of the old style team shifted away into other IP's or are mainly working within Team 3 on FFXIV and FFXVI (and occasionally FFXI updates). Final Fantasy 7 Remake project covers the VII world and FFXVII will likely go somewhere closer to FFXV/VII or something new entirely. If they were to create a Final Fantasy "Classic" spin off series it wouldn't make sense since they have Bravely Default, Octopath Traveller and several smaller titles. As for a main installment getting a classical turn base? I don't see it happening ever again purely based on demographics. Younger generations generally avoid turn base strategy systems now in favour of fast paced, action/ARPG setups (Genshin, Elden Ring, Spiderman, God of War and etc) with the turn base market shrinking down heavily outside of tactical series. To appeal to the wider audience they generally default to Action now and standardise turn base for mobile titles. They also tried it with WoFF and outside of the initial burst the game did somewhat poor numbers overall so they abandoned it for the main console titles.
